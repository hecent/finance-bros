Welcome to University Life Simulator

Think of this as a survival game, but instead of fighting dragons, you are battling essay deadlines, budgeting your student loan, and trying to get enough sleep. Welcome to St. Andrews. Here is your official guide to surviving your degree.

The Core Stats

You have three main statistics to balance. If any of these drop too low, your university career could be in serious trouble.

1. Balance (Money)

You start Year 1 with £1000. At the start of each new academic year, you will receive a fresh stipend of £1000. Every night out, takeaway meal, or expensive textbook will drain this account. Run out of money, and you will be forced to make tough, high-stress choices.

2. Happiness

You start at 50 out of 100. University is stressful. If you only study and never take a break, this stat will tank. You need to engage in hobbies, attend society events, and socialize to keep your morale high.

3. Grades

Your academic standing is graded out of 20. Every year, you start with a clean slate at exactly 10 points. Your goal is to push this score as high as possible. The game classifies your grades into the following UK degree tiers:

  • 16.5 or higher: First Class
  • 13.5 to 16.4: Upper Second Class (2:1)
  • 10.5 to 13.4: Lower Second Class (2:2)
  • 7.0 to 10.4: Third Class
  • Below 7.0: Fail

How to Win

There is no perfect path. If you only focus on grinding for a First Class degree, your happiness will plummet. If you spend all your money on VIP concert tickets, you will not be able to afford emergency expenses like a broken laptop.

Your goal is steady progression. Treat your stats like a delicate ecosystem. Make smart choices, manage your resources, and try to make it to graduation with a solid degree, a positive bank balance, and your sanity intact. Good luck!
